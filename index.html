My Ironman Experience

Date Posted: 04.14.24

I completed my first triathlon in 2019 and immediately became hooked. I hopped on my computer post-race and started looking for my next one. I decided that my next goal would be to finish an Ironman, a triathlon with a 2.4 mile swim, 112 mile bike, a 26.2 mile run. Fast forward a few years due to the COVID-19 pandemic and I finally completed one: Ironman California in Sacramento, California.

Two of the most important things I did to prepare for the race were:

  • First, I purchased a triathlon bike. I completed my first triathlon with a regular road bike and while that was sufficient for a shorter distance race, having a proper triathlon bike can make a huge difference in how much energy you can save due to the efficiency gains.
  • Next, I hired a triathlon coach. I had no idea where to start with training and preparing for a race of this distance, thankfully I found a coach that lives in the same city as me. Very convenient! His expertise and guidance has helped me improve across all three disciplines and was a major reason why I was able to finish the race.

After training for almost a year, I flew to California a week before the race to acclimate to weather and time zone. I spent the week familiarizing myself with the course and getting in my final few workouts. The week flew by and before I knew it was time for race day. My partner and best friend flew out to watch me and their support helped to keep me from bailing at the last minute due to nerves.

Race day came and started with the final checks of all my gear and nutrition for the day. I made my way to the swim start, anxiously awaiting the long day ahead. The gun went off and after few minutes I was able to start the race. It was a long and challenging day but after 13 hours and 40 minutes I crossed the finish line. To this day it is one of my biggest accomplishments.
